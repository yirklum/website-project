Live from DC…

During the Presidential Inauguration of 1953, Walter Cronkite, Chief Washington correspondent for CBS-TV, broadcast live from the newly developed “mobile television transmitting unit”.

Crammed into the body of a stock Packard convertible, a cameraman (right side of vehicle) sends a signal to a transmission dish (left side of vehicle). The transmission dish then transmits the signal to a receiver in the dome of the Capitol.
After receiving the pictures, the signal is routed to the basement of the Capitol and then relayed, by cable, to WTOP-TV studios in Washington for transmission to the CBS-TV network. The plans called for the mobile unit to travel along side President Dwight Eisenhower’s limousine, up Pennsylvania Ave., showing the President and the crowds as the parade traveled from the White House to the Capitol.
