After spending eight years as Vice-President in the Reagan administration, George Herbert Walker Bush became the Republican nominee for the office of President of the United States. He chose Sen Dan Quayle, from Indiana, as his running mate. Bush fought off challenges from US Senator Bob Dole of Kansas and televangelist Pat Robertson for the nomination. The Democratic Party selected Massachusetts Governor Michael Dukakis as their standard bearer along with their Vice-Presidential pick, US Senator Lloyd Bentsen from Texas. Dukakis beat out US Senator Gary Hart after the Colorado Senator was caught in a sex scandal with Donna Rice. He was also helped when popular Sen Ted Kennedy decided not to run.
